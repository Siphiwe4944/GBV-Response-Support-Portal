Legal Resources

Understanding your legal rights and available legal protections

Protection Orders

A protection order is a legal document that can help protect you from further abuse or harassment.

Types of Protection Orders:
  • Interim Protection Order: Immediate temporary protection
  • Final Protection Order: Long-term protection after court hearing
How to Apply:
  1. Go to your nearest Magistrate's Court
  2. Complete Form 2 (application for protection order)
  3. Submit supporting evidence if available
  4. Attend court hearing if required

Your Legal Rights

Right to Safety

You have the right to live free from violence and abuse

Right to Legal Protection

You can seek protection orders and legal remedies

Right to Legal Aid

Free legal assistance is available through Legal Aid SA

Right to Privacy

Your case can be heard in private chambers

Criminal vs Civil Proceedings

Criminal Case
  • State prosecutes the accused
  • You are a witness, not the complainant
  • Can result in imprisonment or fine
  • Must be reported to police
Civil Case
  • You take action against the accused
  • Can claim damages
  • Protection orders are civil matters
  • Lower burden of proof required

Emergency Legal Help

If you need immediate legal protection:

  1. Go to your nearest Magistrate's Court
  2. Ask for the clerk of the court
  3. Request an interim protection order
  4. Bring any evidence you have
